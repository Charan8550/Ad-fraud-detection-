About Ad Click Fraud Detection Project

Advanced Click Fraud Detection Using Machine Learning

This study addresses the challenge of predicting mobile app downloads following advertisement clicks, a task crucial for detecting click fraud and optimizing ad targeting. The dataset comprises user interactions with mobile ads, featuring attributes such as IP, device, OS, app ID, channel, and click timestamps. The target variable is is_attributed, indicating whether the app was downloaded after a click. To capture complex behavioral patterns, we applied a range of supervised learning algorithms including Logistic Regression, Decision Tree, Random Forest, KNN, Naïve Bayes, Gradient Boosting, LightGBM, and XGBoost. Deep learning models such as ANN, DNN, CNN, RNN, LSTM, and GRU were also employed for sequential and high-dimensional feature learning. A stacking classifier was constructed to leverage the strengths of multiple models. Feature engineering and timestamp-based pattern analysis were key in enhancing performance. This multi-model approach aids in accurately distinguishing genuine user clicks from fraudulent ones, ensuring ad campaign integrity and better conversion tracking.
